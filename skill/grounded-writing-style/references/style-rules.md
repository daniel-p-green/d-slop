# Style Rules

## Avoid Inflated Meaning

Do not exaggerate symbolism, importance, or emotional weight.

Replace:

- "plays a pivotal role"
- "continues to captivate"
- "highlights"
- "underscores"
- "showcases"
- "serves as a testament to"

With the actual action or fact.

Example:

Bad:

> The workshop underscored the importance of experimentation.

Better:

> People tried the tool, broke a few flows, and left with one workflow they could reuse.

## Avoid Stock Structures

Cut or rewrite these patterns:

- "Despite these challenges..."
- "In conclusion..."
- "In summary..."
- "Overall..."
- "Challenges and future prospects..."
- "From X to Y..."
- "Not only... but also..."

These usually signal that the sentence was arranged before the writer knew what it needed to say.

## Avoid AI Vocabulary

Watch for words that make ordinary claims sound synthetic:

- align
- crucial
- delve
- elaborate
- emphasize
- enhance
- enduring
- foster
- garner
- highlight
- intricate
- interplay
- pivotal
- showcase
- tapestry
- underscore

Do not ban these mechanically. Some are fine in technical or quoted contexts. Treat them as warning lights.

## Prefer Concrete Detail

Look for:

- names
- dates
- numbers
- tools
- files
- places
- physical details
- decisions
- tradeoffs
- failure modes

When the source lacks details, keep the sentence modest.

Bad:

> This powerful solution transformed the team's workflow.

Better:

> The script turned a 20-minute cleanup into a two-command check.

## No Meta Framing

Do not open with:

- "Let's walk through..."
- "Below is a detailed overview..."
- "This article explores..."
- "In this guide, we will..."

Start with the point.

## No Obvious Lessons

Cut lines like:

- "It is important to remember that..."
- "This reminds us that..."
- "The key takeaway is..."

If a takeaway is needed, make it specific:

> Ship the small check first. It catches the bad drafts before they get dressed up.

## Pacing

Use short sentences for judgment.

Use longer sentences when a sentence has real work to do: context, contrast, or a concrete sequence.

Do not create a false rhythm with three padded phrases.

## Formatting

Keep the page clean.

- Use bullets for real lists.
- Use headings for navigation, not decoration.
- Avoid stacked sections that all have the same length.
- Avoid endings that summarize what the reader just read.
